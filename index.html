Abstract

This paper presents GOMP (Grasped Object Manifold Projection), a novel approach for multimodal imitation learning of manipulation tasks. Our method leverages manifold projection techniques to learn robust manipulation policies that can generalize across different object shapes and grasping configurations. By projecting high-dimensional sensory inputs onto a learned object manifold, GOMP enables efficient learning from demonstrations while maintaining the ability to adapt to novel objects and scenarios. We demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach on a variety of manipulation tasks, showing significant improvements in both learning efficiency and generalization performance compared to existing methods.
